What's TKC?

Tokyo Karate Club is an official branch of the Japan Karatedo Federation Wado-kai with 1,350 domestic branches, 250 overseas, and 1.85 million members.

Wado-ryu was strongly influenced by Jiu-Jitsu (Shinto-Youshin-ryu) and has inherited defensive body movements and techniques such as "sabaki," "nagasi," "oshi," "hiki," "irimi," and "tenshin."

The techniques that bring together the wisdom of the Japanese ancients allow people from the elderly to children to participate in the lessons safely

Why TKC?

Because the training method is not usual!

Tokyo Karate Club provides you to Scientific Karate, analyzing a motion one step at a time to think about the meanings of the motion deeply.

Let's try the following Qs to test how much you've thought about Karate deeply and scientifically!

Can you answer these Qs?

A1. No, you shouldn't.

The muscle-straining leads to slow motions. Aren't you mistaken that you need to clench your fists tightly, especially in Kata? Please have a try to do Kata by unclenching them. You'll find that your tsuki and keri become faster and flexible like a whip. This motion is the true technique in Karate. If you'd like to know more, join in TKC.

A2. Your waist, hips, lower-back

Many people misunderstand that they need move their arms or legs quickly. However, the straining of arms or legs make their punches or kickes slow. The waist, hips, or lower-back is kosi (腰) in Japanese kanji. As can be seen, 腰 can be divided into 月 and 要. Because 月 means human body and 要 means pivot, 腰 (waist, hips, or lower-back) is a vital point of your body. If you'd like to know more, join in TKC.
